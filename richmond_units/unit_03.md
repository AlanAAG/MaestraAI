## Lesson Group 1-4

### Early Learning Goals

- To identify and describe wild animals: singular and plural
- To describe ongoing actions
- To trace actions
- To correct negative sentences
- To count sets of animals
- To add two sets of animals together

### Vocabulary

safari, elephant, zebra, lion, giraffe, hippo, crocodile, rhino, bird, monkey, tiger, gorilla, snake, drinking, climbing, walking, eating, flying, swimming, sleeping

### Language Models

- What's this? It's an elephant. What are these? They are hippos. (This/That) is an elephant. (These/Those) are elephants.
- What's this? What's it doing? It's climbing. The little brown monkey is climbing.
- Is it eating? Yes, it is. / No, it isn't. Is the monkey climbing? Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.
- The bird isn't eating. Are (the/those) rhinos swimming? Yes, they are. / No, they aren't. How many lions are there? There are two lions. How many animals altogether? Two plus three equals five.

## Lesson Group 5-8

### Early Learning Goals

- To identify wild animals and say how they move
- To identify and trace actions
- To follow directions
- To describe the locations of animals
- To identify and trace prepositions

### Vocabulary

jungle, running, swinging, hiding, crawling, standing, tiger, gorilla, snake, crocodile, in front of, behind, next to, on, between, plants, tree, rock

### Language Models

- What are these? They are tigers. What are they doing? They're running. What's this? It's a monkey. What's it doing? It's swinging.
- Where's the gorilla? It's next to the tree. Where are the tigers? They're behind the rock. The green crocodile is in front of the plants. Where's the rhino? It's behind the tree. Which animal is next to the rock? The snake is next to the rock.

## Lesson Group 9-12

### Early Learning Goals

- To describe, compare and trace animal characteristics
- To put animal types and characteristics into categories
- To recognize characteristics of animals (in written form)
- To describe, compare and trace animal body parts
- To develop phonemic awareness
- To identify zoo animals and body parts

### Vocabulary

big, small, spots, stripes, tiger, zebra, snake, giraffe, elephant, hippo, mouse, bird, long, short, big, small, ears, tails, legs, crocodile, panda, claws, tail, strong arms, horn, paws, teeth, zoo

### Language Models

- This is a tiger. It has stripes. The tiger and zebra have stripes. The elephant has big ears. Does the giraffe have big ears? Yes, it does. / No, it doesn't.
- This is a lion. It has sharp claws. I'm thinking of an animal. Is it a snake? Yes, it is. / No, it isn't. A lion and a tiger have sharp teeth. Do crocodiles have strong arms? Yes, they do. / No, they don't. What do they have? They have sharp teeth. Which animal has a pointy horn? The rhino has a pointy horn.

## Lesson Group 13-16

### Early Learning Goals

- To identify characters in a story
- To make predictions about what a story is about
- To describe how characters are feeling
- To identify and trace the feeling words
- To compare physical traits of story characters
- To retell a story
- To sequence a story
- To recognize, count and sequence numbers to 20
- To talk about the importance of being kind to others

### Vocabulary

happy, sad, scared, angry, strong, proud, big, small, lion, mouse, friend, hunter, trapped, net, chew, escape, kind, numbers 1–20, be kind, share my lunch, share my crayons, help a friend, share my ball, share my blocks, hurt someone's feelings

### Language Models

- How does the mouse feel? The mouse feels happy. The mouse is running on the lion. Does the lion have a long (short) tail? Yes, it does. (No, it doesn't.) The lion has (doesn't have) a long tail. Is the lion happy or angry? The lion is happy.
- What number is this? It's 10. What comes after 10? Eleven. How does the lion feel? The lion feels sad. What can the mouse do? The mouse can help the lion. Is the mouse kind? Yes, the mouse is kind. What is the yellow dinosaur doing? Is he being kind? Is he sharing his lunch? Yes, he is.
