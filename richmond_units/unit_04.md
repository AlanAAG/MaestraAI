## Lesson Group 1-4

### Early Learning Goals

- To identify, name and trace body parts
- To distinguish right from left and trace the words left and right
- To understand the importance of exercising to stay healthy
- To review and count numbers 1 to 20
- To add and subtract using fingers
- To understand the meaning of +, - and = symbols

### Vocabulary

neck, arm, leg, hand, foot, head, shoulder, elbow, knee, ankle, right, left, numbers to 20, actions (hop, jump, touch, lift, shake, wave, wiggle, clap), adding, subtracting, counting, fingers

### Language Models

- What's this? Neck. Touch your neck. How many elbows do you have? Two. I have two elbows. This is my right hand. Hop on your right foot. Wave your left hand. What's this? This is Kelly's left hand. Jimmy can hop eight times. Can you hop? Yes, I can. How many times? I can hop (five) times.
- Addition: What number is this? Three. Show me three fingers. Three plus two equals five. Subtraction: Show me five fingers. Take away three. How many are left? Two.

## Lesson Group 5-8

### Early Learning Goals

- To understand the importance of protecting one's skin from the sun
- To understand how to dress for different weather conditions
- To describe what someone is wearing
- To identify and differentiate between healthy and unhealthy habits
- To understand the importance of developing healthy habits

### Vocabulary

the weather, sunscreen, clothing, eat healthy food, brush my teeth/hair, exercise, wash my hands, blow my nose/use a tissue, drink lots of water, take a shower, go to bed on time, stay up late, cover your sneeze, play, get rest, do my best

### Language Models

- How's the weather? It's hot and sunny. What's the girl wearing? She's wearing a hat, sunglasses and sunscreen. What do you do to stay healthy? I (eat a healthy breakfast) to stay healthy.
- Do you brush your teeth three times a day? Yes, I do. / No, I don't. Eating an apple is healthy. Eating candy isn't healthy.

## Lesson Group 9-12

### Early Learning Goals

- To talk about how one feels
- To recommend solutions
- To identify and trace feelings words
- To talk about ailments and remedies
- To recommend solutions for getting better after being sick

### Vocabulary

tired, sick, hungry, thirsty, fan your face, put on your jacket, call the doctor, eat a cracker, drink some water, cough, sore throat, stomachache, fever, cold, cover your mouth, use a tissue, rest in bed

### Language Models

- How do you feel? I'm tired. What does he need? He needs to drink water. What's wrong with her? She has (doesn't have) a headache.
- I'm sick. I feel bad. I have a fever. What can I do to get better? I can tell my mom. I take medicine (drink water, rest in bed). What's wrong? I have a cough. Have a drink of water.

## Lesson Group 13-16

### Early Learning Goals

- To differentiate between safe and unsafe practices on the playground
- To retell a story
- To describe common, minor injuries
- To talk about the importance of playing safely with friends on the playground

### Vocabulary

safe, dangerous, be careful, slide, skateboard, bike, helmet, swing, climbing frame, hurt, scraped, broke, cut, bumped, see-saw, take turns, run after a ball, street, ride my bike, jump

### Language Models

- What's this boy doing? He's riding his bike. Wear your helmet when you ride your bike. She's swinging on the swing. Is that safe? Yes. She knows how to swing safely.
- What's he doing? He's jumping off the swing. Don't jump off the swing. I scraped/hurt my knee. The boy scraped his knee. They are playing on the slide. Are they taking turns? Yes. They are taking turns.
