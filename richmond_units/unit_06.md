## Lesson Group 1-4

### Early Learning Goals

- To identify elements of nature
- To differentiate between land and water
- To understand that we share the planet and that we need to take care of it
- To identify animals and classify them by where they live: air, land, water
- To identify and sort natural and man-made objects

### Vocabulary

Planet Earth, water, sea, ocean, land, country, forest, mountain, river, fish, air, lake, trees, bird, deer, fox, squirrel, frog, duck, sky, natural, man-made, leaf, soda can, flower, butterfly, building, ball, clouds, grass

### Language Models

- The Earth is our home. This is the land (water). We live on the land. Animals live on the land, in the sky, and in the water. We share the Earth with the animals. This is natural. It's part of the natural world.
- This is man-made. A person made it.

---

## Lesson Group 5-8

### Early Learning Goals

- To understand that litter is a problem
- To identify and describe the location of trash items
- To understand that we need clean air/water and what air/water pollution is
- To introduce how we can take care of the Earth by reducing, reusing and recycling

### Vocabulary

environment, clean, polluted, air/water pollution, factory litter, glass/plastic bottle, plastic rings, in, on, under, next to, between, sea, lake, pond, river, plastic bags, juice boxes, reduce, reuse, recycle

### Language Models

- We can pick up the litter. It's in the sandbox. It's on the bench. We need clean air to breathe. There is smoke coming from the factories, cars and buses.
- Is the pond polluted? No, it isn't. It's clean. What's polluting the water? A plastic bag. What can we do to help the Earth? We can put litter in the trash. We can use things again.

## Lesson Group 9-12

### Early Learning Goals

- To understand that water and electricity are natural resources and we need them
- To understand the concepts of saving and wasting water and electricity
- To understand the importance of recycling
- To sort trash by type
- To count to 100 by tens

### Vocabulary

water, electricity, use lights, hose, watering can, trash, can, plant a tree, save, waste, recycle, plastic, metal, paper, recycling box, numbers to 100 (by tens), reuse, plastic container

### Language Models

- He's watching TV. He's using electricity/water. Don't run the water when you brush your teeth. It's a newspaper. Which box does the newspaper go into? Paper.
- How many dots are there? 10. How many sets of 10 are there? Three. How many is that? 30.

---

## Lesson Group 13-16

### Early Learning Goals

- To identify sea animals
- To listen to, follow and sequence a story
- To understand the importance of not polluting the ocean/sea
- To recognize the importance of looking for solutions
- To review numbers (multiples of 10 to 100) and colors
- To talk about the importance of taking care of the Earth and sea animals

### Vocabulary

plastic bag, float, dolphins, boat, trash, soda, cooler, sea, sea turtles, pollution, clean, safe, river, whale, shark, jellyfish, starfish, octopus, coral, recycle, sort, turn off the water, plastic bottle, litter, plant a tree

### Language Models

- What happens next? The dolphins are swimming next to the plastic bag. The trash falls in the river. I think the blue whales are going to eat the trash.
- What's this? It's a jellyfish. What color is the jellyfish? Put your finger on the dolphin with number 100. What are the dinosaurs doing? Are they sorting their trash? Yes, they are. / No, they aren't.
