## Lesson Group 1-4

### Early Learning Goals

- To describe hair and eye color
- To develop a concept of self
- To describe hair type and eye color
- To understand the concept that we are all special
- To trace words that describe hair types

### Vocabulary

hair, eyes, brown, black, blond, blue, green, friend, boy, girl, long, short, curly, straight, red, small, big

### Language Models

- What's his/her name? Is he/she a boy or a girl? What color are his/her eyes? What color is his/her hair? I have brown hair and brown eyes.
- My friend has black hair and green eyes. She's a girl. She has curly, red hair. Is her hair long or short? It's long. What color is her hair? It's blond.
- Does this (boy) have (curly) hair? Yes, (he) does. / No, (he) doesn't. He has straight, blond hair and light blue eyes.

## Lesson Group 5-8

### Early Learning Goals

- To identify family members
- To describe family member's physical traits
- To compare one's physical traits to those of a family member
- To trace words that describe physical traits
- To listen to a chant and follow again
- To develop phonemic awareness

### Vocabulary

aunt, uncle, cousin, mother, father, brother, sister, grandpa, grandma, hair color, eye color, glasses, tall, short, fat, thin, young, old, family members, older, younger, taller, shorter, fatter, thinner

### Language Models

- This is my uncle. He has black hair and brown eyes. Tell me about Jimmy's aunt. What's she like? She has blond hair and blue eyes.
- Does Jimmy's father have brown eyes? Yes, he does. / No, he doesn't. Who's she? She's my aunt. What's she like? She has blond hair and blue eyes. Who does she look like? She looks like Jimmy's uncle. Who do you look like? I look like my (mom). She has brown hair. I have brown hair, too.

## Lesson Group 9-12

### Early Learning Goals

- To understand that living things grow
- To sort living and nonliving things
- To sequence people by age
- To trace words that describe people's age
- To compare people by height and age
- To trace words that describe height and age
- To learn to measure objects by centimeters

### Vocabulary

girl, woman, sprout, plant, puppy, dog, book, baby, kitten, robot, living things, grow, baby, boy, man, old man, older, younger, taller, shorter, growing, tall, taller, tallest, short, shorter, shortest, young, younger, youngest, old, older, oldest

### Language Models

- What's this? It's a puppy. Do puppies grow? Yes. They grow up to be dogs. They are living things. Living things grow. Does a book grow? No, it doesn't. It's not a living thing.
- The baby is very young. He is getting older. Who is older (younger, taller), the man or the baby? The man. Who's taller, the mother or the sister? The mother is taller than the sister. Who's the tallest? The father is the tallest.

## Lesson Group 13-16

### Early Learning Goals

- To make predictions about what will happen in a story
- To listen attentively and to follow a story
- To make size comparisons
- To trace words that describe size
- To retell a story
- To sequence events in a story
- To trace sequence words
- To talk about the importance of helping our siblings and family members

### Vocabulary

little, medium, big, goats, horses, oldest, fattest, thinnest, biggest, smallest, billy goats, bridge, river, troll, grass, first, then, next, finally, help my sibling get dressed, go to the park with my sibling, keep my sibling safe, play with my sibling, feed my sibling

### Language Models

- What color is the little goat? White. Who's bigger (fatter, thinner, older, younger) the (medium) goat or the (little) goat? Who's this? Little Billy Goat. Who's the oldest (youngest, fattest, thinnest)?
- First, ... What happened? Then, ... What happened next? Next, ... What happened after that? Finally, ... What's Dino (Dino's sister) doing? Is he (she) helping his (her) sibling? Yes! He's (She's) helping his (her) baby brother get dressed. Dino (Dino's sister) is helping his (her) sibling.
