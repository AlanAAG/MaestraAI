## Lesson Group 1-4

### Early Learning Goals

- To say one's name and to greet others
- To identify and say daily classroom routines
- To say which school activities one can do
- To say one's gender and age
- To describe school routines one likes or doesn't like to do
- To say what Dino's favorite things are
- To describe one's favorite things

### Vocabulary

sing, speak English, write, count, cut, draw, color, boy, girl, listen to stories, clean up, food, colors, toys, friend, play, paint

### Language Models

- What's your (his/her) name? My (His/Her) name is (Dino).
- Where is (Jimmy)? He's / She's not here.
- What can Jimmy and Kelly do at school? They can sing.
- Are you a boy or a girl? I'm a (boy/girl).
- How old are you? I'm (five) years old.

## Lesson Group 5-8

### Early Learning Goals

- To name the days of the school week
- To trace the days of the school week
- To identify special classes at school
- To review numbers 1–20
- To say when one has special classes and whether he/she likes them
- To learn how to tell time on the hour
- To identify special school activities and times

### Vocabulary

school days, PE, art, computer class, music, science, clock, time, numbers 1–12, days of the school week, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday

### Language Models

- What day is today? Today is Monday.
- When do they have art? They have art on Tuesday.
- Do we have music? Yes, we do. / No, we don't.
- Do you like science? Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
- What time is it? It's six o'clock. Is it 8 o'clock? Yes, it is. / No, it isn't. It's 7 o'clock.

## Lesson Group 9-12

### Early Learning Goals

- To name and trace the days of the school week
- To identify and name weekend (recreational) activities
- To trace days of the weekend
- To say which activities one likes to do on the weekend
- To identify and trace the parts of the day

### Vocabulary

weekday, weekend, Saturday, Sunday, go to the supermarket, visit my grandparents, go to the movies, stay home, play with my pet, play with my toys, play with my friends, watch TV, ride my bike, morning, afternoon, evening

### Language Models

- We go to school (stay home) on (Monday).
- What do you do on the weekend? I visit my grandparents on the weekend.
- Do you like to play with your pet? Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
- When do you watch TV? In the morning, afternoon or evening? I watch TV in the (morning).
- What's Jimmy doing? He's playing with his toys.
- What does Dino do on Wednesdays? He plays video games with his friend.
- Does Dino go to the movies on Tuesday? No, he doesn't.

## Lesson Group 13-16

### Early Learning Goals

- To identify more special classes
- To listen attentively to a story
- To predict what a story is about
- To identify characters and feelings in a story
- To sequence events in a story
- To talk about activities one is good at and a new skill one would like to learn
- To understand the importance of working hard to learn new things

### Vocabulary

karate, science, soccer, swimming, math, love, hate, good at, excited, scared, afraid, angry, bored, proud, sad, ride a bike, play tennis, do karate, play the recorder

### Language Models

- She loves his/her class.
- He/She is good at (soccer).
- I'm (angry).
- What class does Andy have on Wednesday? He has music class on Wednesday.
- What time does he have his soccer class? He has his soccer class at 6 o'clock.
- What's he doing? He's swimming.
- Are you good at swimming? What are you good at? I'm good at (dancing).
