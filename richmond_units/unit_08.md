## Lesson Group 1-4

### Early Learning Goals

- To sort items babies and children use
- To say which activities a baby can and cannot do
- To identify the written words yes and no
- To compare activities a child/baby can do independently
- To understand that students are becoming more independent as they grow

### Vocabulary

handprint, bed, crib, shoe, toy, rattle, changing, growing, cry, crawl, write your name, take a bath by yourself, drink from a bottle, use scissors, tie your shoes, brush my hair, get dressed, brush my teeth, put on my socks, grow and change

### Language Models

- This is a picture of me when I was a baby. That was a long time ago. It's a rattle. A baby uses a rattle. Babies can't write but children can. Babies can sit and sleep in their cribs.
- Children can read books. What could Dino do when he was a baby? (He could) sleep in a crib.

## Lesson Group 5-8

### Early Learning Goals

- To talk about new technology and modern devices
- To talk about technology and devices used in the past
- To compare and sort modern and old devices
- To match the same types of devices from the present and the past

### Vocabulary

laptop, tablet, TV, video games, cell phone, video call, long ago, now, telephone, typewriter, record player, radio, new, past, present, game, old toy, old camera, old board game, remote toy, selfie, digital music

### Language Models

- Kelly's calling grandma on the computer. He's playing a game on the tablet. This is Grandma when she was six. That was long ago. This was grandma's TV. She didn't have a computer but she had a typewriter.
- These are things grandma used in the past. These are things we use in the present.

## Lesson Group 9-12

### Early Learning Goals

- To say the days of the week in order
- To understand the concept of yesterday, today and tomorrow
- To understand a schedule of events
- To understand that we change and grow
- To talk about things a robot can do
- To use imagination to create a robot

### Vocabulary

days of the week, music, PE, science, computer class, stay home, past, present, future, yesterday, today, tomorrow, baby, adult, grown-up, robot, play, fly, do homework, clean, talk, dance

### Language Models

- Dino had music class yesterday. He has art class today. They have art class on Tuesday. This is me when I was a baby. This is me now.
- This is what I think I'll look like when I grow up. What can his robot do? It can talk. Can it do homework? Yes, it can. Can it swim? No, it can't.

## Lesson Group 13-16

### Early Learning Goals

- To answer questions about a story
- To discuss children's chores and routines
- To make predictions and talk about life in the future
- To understand that as we grow and change, we can do new things
- To reflect on what has been learned at school this year and make plans for next year

### Vocabulary

crash, helmet, spaceship, buttons, robot, chores, clean your room, do your homework, brush your teeth, futuristic car/refrigerator, robot cleaner, robot dog, enjoy my work, try my best, share my ideas, work with others, this year, next year

### Language Models

- Judy meets Peter's parents on Planet X. He's brushing his teeth. What chore does he have to do? He has to clean his room. It's a futuristic car. What does it do? It flies in the sky.
- What can you do now that you couldn't do when you were a baby? I can (count to 20). How many of you enjoyed your work in this class? Raise your hands. Circle the third star if you really enjoyed your work in this class.
